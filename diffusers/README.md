# Diffusers

I created the models in this directory to diffuse the light from a Hex Unit onto
a hemispherical surface. They are provided for reference in making your own
work.

## Flat Diffuser

As a fit test for later designs, and to reduce the strain when viewing test
animations for a long time, I created a flat diffuser that clips onto the Hex
Unit.  These need to be printed in something that's at least partly transparent.

## Hemispherical Diffusers

Each hemispherical diffuser consists of a base and a shell. There are two pairs
of bases and shells, one for convex uses, and one for concave. The diameter of
the shells is different for the convex and concave models, you'll need to print
the one that matches the base you've selected.

For my work, I printed the bases in black filament to reduce bleed-over between
pixels. If you want a glowing diffused effect, white filament works well for
that.

The shells should be printed in semi-transparent filament.  You don't need to
purchase anything special, inexpensive "natural" white PETG and PLA worked fine
for me.

In early attempts, I printed the outer shells with the large opening face down
on the print plate. This resulted in a concentric pattern of layer lines that
acted as a kind of lens.  In later work, I started printing the outer shells
with the opening oriented vertically. Although this means you'll need support
structure, the results are stronger and the layer lines have less of a lensing
effect and more of a subtle interlacing effect.
